DOBA-Net
=
Accurate medical image segmentation is essential for computer-assisted diagnosis and precision treatment. However, precise segmentation of organs or lesions faces numerous challenges, such as blurred boundaries and low tissue contrast, among which the accurate delineation of boundaries between target regions and surrounding tissues is particularly demanding. To address these issues, we proposes a Differential Operator-Driven Boundary-Aware Network (DOBA-Net), specifically designed for complex medical images segmentation tasks. By integrating differential operator theory with modern attention mechanisms, the network explicitly models gradient and curvature features to effectively preserve high-frequency boundary features and suppress their attenuation and smoothing. DOBA-Net adopts an end-to-end architecture comprising three core modules: the Differential Operator Boundary Extractor (DOBE), which captures gradient and curvature features through learnable Gaussian smoothing, phase-rotated Sobel operators, and Hessian operator curvature computation; the Bidirectional Attention Gating module (BAG), which receives boundary priors at the encoder stage and achieves deep interaction between boundary features and backbone features through a forward-reverse dual-branch mechanism; and the Adaptive Feature-Edge Fusion module (AFEF), which progressively fuses encoder features with corresponding semantic edge maps generated by DOBE at each layer, ensuring precise preservation of boundary features during feature reconstruction. Experimental results on four public datasets, namely ISIC2017, MoNuSeg2018,PH2 and COVID-19, demonstrate that the proposed DOBA-Net outperforms other popular models in terms of Dice, HD95, and IoU.
